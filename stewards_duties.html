A Scalable Framework

Based on agency feedback, we are focused on flexible, high-impact engagements. While long-term exchanges are a goal, our primary focus is on short-term consultations and projects in high-demand areas like IT, HR, Finance, and Procurement.

TIER 1

Shadowing

A short-term, observational experience. Staff visit a peer agency to gain insight without disrupting daily operations.

TIER 2

Consulting & Projects

Time-limited deployment of a Subject Matter Expert (SME) to solve a defined problem, consult on an RFP, or assist with a specific project. Engagements can range from a few days to several weeks, part-time or full-time.

TIER 3

Strategic Exchange

A high-impact, full-time placement (e.g., 3-6 months) to lead a transformation or transfer institutional knowledge. This tier requires detailed planning and agency head approval.

Addressing Key Questions

Transparency is a core part of our process. Here's how we're addressing common concerns.

My agency is already understaffed. How can we afford to lend anyone out?

We recognize this is the #1 challenge. The tiered system is designed for this exact reason. We've found most agencies prefer short-term, high-flexibility options.

Instead of a long-term "loan," consider a Tier 2 Consultation: lending your expert for just a few days or even part-time for a specific project. Tier 1 (Shadowing) is also a low-disruption way to participate. Tier 3 is an admirable goal, not the expected starting point.

What about employees funded by different sources (non-general fund, grant-funded, etc.)?

This is a critical detail. The Memorandum of Agreement (MOA) between agencies is the key tool to solve this. The MOA will explicitly define the conditions of the engagement, including any cost-sharing and considerations for the employee's specific funding source (e.g., grant requirements) to ensure full compliance.

How do we prevent "dual workload," where an employee feels they have to do two jobs at once?

This is a major failure point of past informal efforts, and a core problem the Connector is designed to solve. The MOA and Expectation Checklists are mandatory and must be signed by all parties *before* the engagement begins.

These documents will explicitly state whether the engagement is full-time (home agency duties are fully backfilled) or part-time (e.g., 10 hours/week) with clear, agreed-upon deliverables. This protects the employee, the sending agency, and the receiving agency.

What about logistical hurdles like VITA access, IT permissions, and building access?

These logistical issues are precisely why longer-term exchanges (Tier 3) require significant planning. However, for shorter Tier 1 and Tier 2 engagements, access is often simpler.

For example, a "Shadowing" employee may only need a guest badge. A "Consulting" expert may be able to provide all deliverables using their own equipment and email, without needing deep access to the host's network. The MOA will define what access is required and the timeline for provisioning it.
